# Smart-VLSI-and-WBG-Devices
This repository contains the coursework and project material developed for the **Wide Band Gap Semiconductor Devices** course. The project focuses on the physics, device structures, and applications of wide band gap materials such as **Silicon Carbide (SiC)** and **Gallium Nitride (GaN)**.

Wide band gap semiconductors are enabling technologies for next-generation **power electronics**, **high frequency devices**, and **high temperature applications**.

---

## Project Objectives

The main goals of this project are:

- Study the fundamental properties of wide band gap semiconductor materials.
- Understand the device physics of **SiC and GaN based devices**.
- Analyze their advantages over conventional silicon devices.
- Investigate real-world applications in **power electronics and RF systems**.
- Explore device modeling and simulation techniques.

---

## Wide Band Gap Semiconductors

Wide band gap (WBG) materials have a larger band gap than conventional silicon, typically greater than **2 eV**. These materials offer several advantages:

- Higher breakdown voltage
- Higher operating temperature
- Higher switching frequency
- Lower conduction losses

Common WBG materials include:

- Silicon Carbide (SiC)
- Gallium Nitride (GaN)
- Diamond (experimental)
- Aluminum Nitride (AlN)

---

## Applications

Wide band gap semiconductor devices are widely used in:

- Electric vehicle power converters
- Renewable energy systems
- High frequency RF amplifiers
- Power grid infrastructure
- Aerospace and defense electronics
